IC Card App Concept

This app was created by me alone as a concept only and has no relation to the organizations mentioned.
For this case study, I chose to create a concept for a public transportation card application.
I conducted research, created mockups, and finally, created high fidelity prototypes to actualize my ideas.

Introduction

"Ugh, where did I put my card?!"

Many people throughout Japan use an IC card on a daily basis. IC cards allow people to pay public transportation with a simple tap of a prepaid card on a scanner. Some places even allow IC cards to be used at various merchants such as convenience stores, so as long as you have your card on you, you're good to go! At their core, they sound like an amazing daily convenience. However, one thing about them goes against that very principle.

They can't be recharged online.

The Research

Remote surveys were collected

In order to best understand my target demographic, I conducted research on those who were both currently living in Japan and users of public transportation. I'm quite glad that I chose to do this, because I learned of some other key features that people would want and would not need. For example, I learned that many people would not be interested in smart watch integration, so it's probably best that I don't make that a priority. However, they are very interested in using it to check stations and find out if certain cards are accepted near them. Here are some sample answers that I gathered in my survey. Other questions given were basic demographic questions, such as age, time spent in Japan, what cards they use, and when/how they travel.

Competition Analysis

I found two apps that had a similar purpose in the Japanese application market. The first is the Suica Mobile App and the other is SmartEX, a bullet train ticket reservation app. By studying the current market competition, I realized that there really is a need for a more wide-spread applicable transit card app that can handle both daily transportation as well as longer-trips, leading to bullet train ticket integration.

Mobile Suica
Pros

  • Bullet train ticket integration
  • Works with smart watches
Cons

  • Japanese language only
  • Works with older androids, or simply Apple Pay/Google Pay
  • Suica Region Only
SmartEX
Pros

  • Available in both Japanese and English
  • Can register IC card for easy boarding
Cons

  • Bullet train only
  • Difficult to check train times
  • Some credit cards not accepted
Planning Phase

With a pen and my favorite notebook, I set out to plan the project.

In the mockups below, I mapped out what I expected the user flow to be, and organized which screens I would need to design. I imagined that a user would want to use the app quickly on-the-go, which helped lead to the final product. I also planned the task bar and made myself notes to be sure that I kept good design principles in mind the entire time.

The Process

Low and High Fidelity Mockups

Using Figma, I was able to collaborate with potential users remotely and get very quick feedback on the positives and negatives of my design's user exerpience. The hardest thing that I struggled with in this project was the organization and design of the tool bar. It's something that many of us use regularly but rarely actually think about. Because I wanted to get this right, I relied heavily on feedback from others to be sure that things felt natural and intuitive.

Current Status

The project in it's current state

Throughout the process, some feedback that I received really stuck with me and affected the way I arrived at this final launch screen. I was told to think about the situations in which a user may want to access this application. They'll probably be on the go and their main concern will be to very simply recharge their card. Thus, the quick charge front screen was born! I created sliders for the common recharge amounts. These amounts were copied from the card recharge machines at stations, so they are familiar to users. Then, I made it a two step process, to help users who may be afraid of accidentally charging the wrong amount. You slide, press the lightning bolt to charge, and then confirm the payment. It's simple, yet secure, and creates a great user experience.
