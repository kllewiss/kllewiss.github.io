IC Card App

For this case study, I chose to create a concept for a public transportation card application.
I conducted research, created mockups, and finally, created high fidelity prototypes to actualize my ideas.

The Main Problem

Keeping track of IC cards is inconvenient and stressful

Many people throughout Japan use an IC card on a daily basis. IC cards allow people to pay public transportation with a simple tap of a prepaid card on a scanner. Some places even allow IC cards to be used at various merchants such as convenience stores, so as long as you have your card on you, you're good to go! At their core, they sound like an amazing daily convenience. However, one thing about them goes against that very principle.
They can't be recharged online.

Other Problems I Found

Can't Track Expenses

Many people want to be able to monitor their spending easily! This is currently a pain to deal with, as it'd require you to keep every receipt you get at the train station.

Can't remember card

Some people have trouble remembering to carry their card on them. If you do forget your card, you end up paying more money because you must buy the tickets there.

Can't understand stations

Navigating the railways and understanding which stations take which cards can be confusing at times!

Can't use credit cards

Many people want to be able to pay for things by credit card, but currently, charge stations only take cash.

The Research

Survey conducted to gather insight into target audience

I know that I, myself, would love to use this app to make my life easier! But, I still chose to gather some data before planning out the major features of the app. I'm quite glad that I chose to do this, because I learned of some other key features that people would want and would not need. For example, I learned that many people would not be interested in smart watch integration, so it's probably best that I don't make that a priority. However, they are very interested in using it to check stations and find out if certain cards are accepted near them. Here are some sample answers that I gathered in my survey. Other questions given were basic demographic questions, such as age, time spent in Japan, what cards they use, and when/how they travel.

Planning Phase

With only a pen and paper, I began planning the project.

In the mockups below, you can see that I first simply used a pen and paper to map out what I expected the user flow to be, as well as organize which screens I would need to design. I also planned the task bar and made myself notes to be sure that I kept good design principles in mind the entire time.

The Process

I created mockups and prototyped the user flow using Figma

Figma is an amazing tool for me, as I love to create concepts and then share them with others using the present tool. Using this, I was able to collaborate and get very quick feedback on the positives and negatives of my design. The hardest thing that I struggled with in this project was the organization and design of the tool bar! It's something that many of us use regularly but rarely actually think about. Because I wanted to get this right, I relied heavily on feedback from others to be sure that things felt natural and intuitive.
