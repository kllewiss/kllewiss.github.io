IC Charge is an app that was created to support the use of domestic transit cards in Japan. The app allows users of Japanese public transportation to add multiple cards to a virtual wallet, monitor spending, and add funds on-the-go. These are features which are currently unavailable.

Project Type

Product Design (user research, planning, design, testing)

Project Length

3 months

Tools Used

Typeform Survey, Figma, Invision
skip to the solution

The Problem

Transit card users struggle to keep track of their expenses and balances

Many people throughout Japan use an IC card, a ticket replacing prepaid transit card, on a daily basis, myself included. Not only do IC cards allow people to pay public transportation with a simple tap of a  on a scanner, they have also become similar to a prepaid debit card, as they are accepted like cash at select stores as well.

These cards are intended to be a daily convenience for users. However, one of the greatest problems with the system is that there is no modern application or online service to recharge each IC card on the nationwide network.

User Research

I reached out to my target demographic to learn about their daily habits and issues with the current system.

I created a survey and administered it to people who were both currently living in Japan and use public transportation. Through these surveys, I learned about the key features that people would want and what their biggest pain points are while using IC cards.

Use public transit to travel in general

Almost all of the respondents use buses or trains to get around on occasion

Use their card(s) at least once a week

Many said they use their IC card for transportation at least once a week

Find it a pain to recharge

Many complained about the inconvenience created when they forget to recharge funds

The Competition

I conducted an analysis of two competing applications

I found two apps that had a similar purpose in the Japanese application market.

The first is the Suica Mobile App, which covers the Tokyo-area IC Card through Apple/Google pay, and the other is SmartEX, a bullet train ticket reservation app.

By studying the current market competition, I realized that there really is a need for a more nationwide transit card app that can handle daily transportation, as the mobile application created to serve the bullet train system is already integrated with these transit cards.

Brainstorming & Planning

I created user flow sketches and wireframes to brainstorm ideas

I mapped out what I expected the user flow to be and organized the list of screens needed for an MVP. I imagined that a user would want to use the app quickly and easily on-the-go, which helped lead to the final product. I also planned out the necessary components for the task bar and brainstormed additional features.

The solution

As an insider in my target demographic, I really did understand the needs and concerns of my users. They'll probably be rushing to catch the morning train on their commute and their main concern will be to swiftly recharge their card as they run up to the platforms.

The sliders on the main screen were a result of input from potential users. It's quick to use but safe enough to not worry about accidental charging. These amounts were copied from the card recharge machines at stations, so they are familiar to users. You slide, press the lightning bolt to charge, and then confirm the payment.

Things I learned

I learned the importance of reaching out to others and hearing a wide variety of feedback while designing. Being able to anticipate the feelings and thoughts of others is beneficial, however it can be dangerous relying on your own "intuition" without any clear validation of these feelings. It's imperative to reach out to others and get actual feedback on your ideas.

What I plan to do next

In the future, with this project, I would like to be able to conduct some larger scale in-person user-testing. My sample set for the project was small and mostly remote, so that was flaw in the testing phase.

Also, I would like to continue to improve my UI design skills and return to the project to create more screens, such as the confirmation screen, and improve the visual hierarchy of the main screen.
